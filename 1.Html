Why we exist

Built for the AP class our region never got.

There's no AP Biology, Chemistry, or Studio Art nearby — so students built the version they wished existed. It's real academic rigor with your hands actually in the material, and it happens to build the kind of concrete, talk-about-it-in-an-interview portfolio that matters later, without ever feeling like test prep.

Science + art, actually merged

Models shouldn't just be memorized. Sculpting a lung or wiring a balloon diaphragm builds understanding a worksheet can't.

Real presenting practice

Every student teaches the room a 10-minute slice of the topic — the exact skill that carries into college interviews and beyond.

A portfolio worth keeping

Nine months, nine exhibits. By spring you'll have a shelf of real work and a stack of talks you actually gave.

Week 1 · goal

Brainstorm & project sketching

The club votes on this month's topic and splits into small teams. Everyone starts reading and sketching what their build might look like.

✅ Vote on the month's topic
✅ Form teams of 2–4
✅ First sketches of each build
✅ Assign presentation angles
Week 2 · goal

Presentation week

Each team gives a 10-minute presentation on their slice of the topic. Everyone leaves knowing the whole subject, not just their piece of it.

✅ 10-minute team talks
✅ Peer Q&A after each one
✅ Notes for the studio week
✅ Materials list finalized
Week 3 · goal

Studio week

Free-form making time — clay, balloons, canvas, wire, whatever the topic calls for. This is the week nobody wants to leave early.

✅ Sculpting / building
✅ Painting & finishing
✅ Group crit halfway through
✅ Prep exhibit labels
Week 4 · goal

Showcase & exhibition

A short public showcase of everyone's work — sometimes with a visiting scientist or artist to see what the room built.

✅ Set up the exhibit wall
✅ Short public walkthroughs
✅ Guest Q&A, if scheduled
✅ Vote on next month's topic
